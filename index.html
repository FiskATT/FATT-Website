Our History

In 2006, the Fisk Altitude Achievement Missile Team (FAAMT) was founded to design and build high-power rockets for the NASA Student Launch Initiative (SLI). For nearly two decades, the team proudly represented Fisk University in SLI competitions. In 2024, the team expanded its mission by joining the National Eclipse Ballooning Project, deploying high-altitude balloons to observe the solar eclipses of 2024 and 2025.

With this broader focus, the team evolved into the Fisk Aerospace Technology Team (FAAT), incorporating unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) alongside rockets and balloons. Now, in the 2025–2026 academic year, FAAT is returning to its roots in high-power rocketry, setting its sights on building a vehicle capable of reaching 50,000 feet above ground level—an achievement no HBCU has accomplished before.
